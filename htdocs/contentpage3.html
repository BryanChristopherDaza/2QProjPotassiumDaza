The Consequences

As mentioned in the last part, no surprise rewards were given to the people of the community. Since the rewards did not meet the expectations of the players of the game, many people started to do various things in order for MiHoYo to give better rewards. One of them was the 1-star rating tantrum at Google Play Store.

Many angry players decided to rate the game with 1
star because of the lack of anniversary rewards. This then resulted in the lowest star rating the game has experienced so far. Although there are still people who defend MiHoYo, it was not as dominant as the unsatisfied players. The rating of the game in Google Play Store got as low as 1.9 stars, compared to the previous rating of 4.5 stars. People began to talk about this as some people were afraid that the game will be deleted if the star rating is low enough, which is not true.

Although this seems like an immature way to pursue MiHoYo on giving the players more rewards for the anniversary, it makes some sense. 10 Intertwined Fates is a very little number of fates, considering that a guaranteed limited 5-star character (highest rarity) takes 180 Intertwined Fates. The web events mentioned earlier consisted of a raffle in which 10% of the players who join that raffle get a Blessing of the Welkin Moon (a purchase you can make in the game to get rewards), which is equal to 2700 Primogems, which is just enough to get 16 Intertwined Fates. Considering you won the raffle, along with the anniversary rewards, it would still be only 26 wishes, which is just barely more than 1/9 the number of Intertwined Fates you need to get a guaranteed limited 5-star character. Although 180 Intertwined Fates is the number of Fates that will guarantee you a limited 5-star character, it is unlikely to get to such a high number.
